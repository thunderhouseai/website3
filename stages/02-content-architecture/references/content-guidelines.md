# Content guidelines

## Voice mechanics

- Extract cadence from the founder's samples in brand.md — mirror sentence length
  distribution and vocabulary register. Do not smooth his voice into generic
  agency-speak.
- Plain claims over superlatives. "We turn your workflows into governed AI
  operating systems" beats "cutting-edge AI transformation."
- Second person for the visitor ("your operations"), first person plural for
  ThunderHouse ("we build").
- The philosophy line ("AI is not the strategy — solving business problems is")
  should appear once, prominently, verbatim. Never paraphrased in weaker form.

## Structure rules

- Headlines ≤ 8 words. Straplines: exactly three keywords, comma-separated,
  matching LOUD's pillar pattern (e.g. "Governed, Measured, Human-led").
- One idea per paragraph. Paragraphs ≤ 60 words on pillar blocks, ≤ 90 elsewhere.
- Every page ends with a single CTA. Site-wide CTA voice: invitation, not
  pressure ("Are you the next?" energy, ThunderHouse wording).

## Hard bans

- No em-dash-chained triads of adjectives. No "unlock", "leverage", "empower",
  "supercharge", "revolutionize", "seamless".
- No claims about specific client results unless the founder supplies them.
- No lorem ipsum anywhere. If information is missing, insert
  `[NEEDS-FOUNDER: question]` and list all such markers at the end of copy.md.
